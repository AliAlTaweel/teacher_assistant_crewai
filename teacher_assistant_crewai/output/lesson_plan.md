**Lesson Plan: Introduction to Fractions for Grade 5 Students**

**Subject Overview:**
Fractions are a fundamental concept in mathematics that help students understand part-whole relationships. In this lesson, students will learn to recognize and write fractions, understand the role of the numerator and denominator, and develop skills to add and compare fractions.

**Learning Objectives:**

1. **Recognize and write fractions**: Students will be able to identify and write fractions in their simplest form, understanding the relationship between the numerator and denominator.
2. **Understand equivalent fractions**: Students will learn to identify and create equivalent fractions, recognizing that different fractions can represent the same value.
3. **Add and compare fractions**: Students will develop skills to add and compare fractions, using cross-multiplication or finding a common denominator.

**Lesson Plan:**

### Introduction / Hook (5 minutes)

* Begin by asking students if they have ever shared a pizza or cake with friends. Ask them to describe how they divided it up.
* Write the fractions on the board, such as 1/2 and 2/4, and ask students to share any equivalent fractions they know.
* Introduce the concept that fractions represent a part of a whole and that the top number (numerator) tells us how many parts we have, while the bottom number (denominator) tells us how many equal parts the whole is divided into.

### Main Content Delivery (20 minutes)

* **Step 1: Understanding Numerators and Denominators** (5 minutes)
	+ Write a few simple fractions on the board, such as 1/2 and 3/4.
	+ Ask students to identify the numerator and denominator for each fraction.
* **Step 2: Equivalent Fractions** (5 minutes)
	+ Provide examples of equivalent fractions, such as 1/2 = 2/4 = 4/8.
	+ Ask students to create their own equivalent fractions and share with the class.
* **Step 3: Adding Fractions** (5 minutes)
	+ Introduce the concept of adding fractions and explain that it's not as simple as adding the numerators and denominators separately.
	+ Use cross-multiplication to add fractions, such as 1/2 + 1/3 = ?
* **Step 4: Comparing Fractions** (5 minutes)
	+ Introduce the concept of comparing fractions and explain that it's not as simple as comparing the numerators and denominators separately.
	+ Use cross-multiplication to compare fractions, such as 1/2 > 1/3 or 1/2 < 3/4.

### Practice / Activity (15 minutes)

* Distribute the "Fraction Fun" worksheet, which includes:
	+ Simple addition and comparison problems, such as 1/2 + 1/3 = ?
	+ Equivalent fraction problems, such as 1/2 = ?/4
	+ Simplification problems, such as 2/4 = ?
* Have students work individually to complete the worksheet.
* Allow students to use calculators or draw pictures to help them solve the problems.

### Review and Wrap-up (5 minutes)

* Review the key concepts learned in the lesson, including equivalent fractions and adding and comparing fractions.
* Ask students to share any challenges they faced or interesting insights they gained.
* Provide a preview of the next lesson, which will focus on simplifying fractions.

**Required Materials or Resources:**

* Whiteboard and markers
* Printed copies of the "Fraction Fun" worksheet
* Calculators (optional)

**Differentiation Strategies:**

* **Advanced Students:**
	+ Provide more challenging equivalent fraction problems, such as 2/3 = ?/6
	+ Ask advanced students to create their own word problems involving adding and comparing fractions
* **Struggling Students:**
	+ Provide extra support by using visual aids, such as fraction circles or diagrams, to help students understand the concepts
	+ Offer one-on-one support or small group instruction to help students complete the worksheet

**Time Estimate:**

* Introduction / Hook: 5 minutes
* Main Content Delivery: 20 minutes
* Practice / Activity: 15 minutes
* Review and Wrap-up: 5 minutes
* Total: 45 minutes

**Quiz: Introduction to Fractions for Grade 5 Students**

**Multiple Choice Questions**

1. What is the top number in a fraction called?
a) Denominator
b) Numerator
c) Whole
d) Part

2. Which of the following fractions is equivalent to 1/2?
a) 2/4
b) 3/6
c) 4/8
d) 1/3

3. When adding fractions, what is the correct step?
a) Add the numerators and keep the denominator the same
b) Add the denominators and keep the numerators the same
c) Use cross-multiplication to find the common denominator
d) Simplify the fractions first

4. What is the role of the denominator in a fraction?
a) It tells us how many equal parts the whole is divided into
b) It tells us how many parts we have
c) It is the whole number
d) It is the numerator

5. Which of the following fractions is not equivalent to 1/2?
a) 2/4
b) 3/6
c) 4/8
d) 3/3

**Short Answer Questions**

1. What is the difference between the numerator and denominator in a fraction?

(Answer should include that the numerator tells us how many parts we have, while the denominator tells us how many equal parts the whole is divided into.)

2. How do you simplify a fraction?

(Answer should include that you divide both the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor.)

3. What is the purpose of finding a common denominator when comparing fractions?

(Answer should include that it allows you to add or compare the fractions accurately.)

**True/False Questions**

1. True or False: The numerator is the bottom number in a fraction.

2. True or False: Simplifying a fraction means making the numerator and denominator larger.

Topic: Introduction to Music Theory — Reading Sheet Music (Beginner)

Key concepts:
- The staff: 5 horizontal lines where notes are placed
- Treble clef (G clef): used for higher-pitched instruments (piano right hand, violin, flute)
- Bass clef (F clef): used for lower-pitched instruments (piano left hand, bass guitar, cello)
- Notes on the treble clef lines (bottom to top): Every Good Boy Does Fine (E, G, B, D, F)
- Notes in the treble clef spaces (bottom to top): FACE
- Note durations: whole note (4 beats), half note (2 beats), quarter note (1 beat), eighth note (0.5 beats)
- Time signature: top number = beats per measure, bottom number = which note gets one beat
- Common time signatures: 4/4 (most common), 3/4 (waltz), 6/8
- Rest symbols: silence for the same durations as notes
- Bar lines divide the staff into measures
- Tempo: speed of the music (BPM — beats per minute)

Common student difficulties:
- Memorizing note names on the lines vs. spaces
- Counting rests accurately
- Understanding that the time signature affects how you count, not the speed

Real-world examples:
- "Happy Birthday" is in 3/4 time
- Most pop songs are in 4/4 time
- Counting beats while clapping along to music
